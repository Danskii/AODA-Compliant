Part 4: Alternate Text

Alternate text, or alt text, is a text based alternative to an image. The goal of effective alternate text is to communicate the information contained in an image to someone who may not be able to see it.

Often a person with a visual impairment will use a screen reader to assist them with navigating through a PDF. When a screen reader is used in combination with a PDF the alternate text embedded in an image is automatically read out loud for the user. In a way, this allows the user to percieve visual information that may otherwise be inacessible.

To make your PDF accessible you need to ensure that all images contain alternate text. Here's how to add alternate text to images in your PDF.

  1. Click on the Accessibility shortcut in the right hand panel
  2. Click on reading order
  3. Open the tags tree on the left hand side
  4. Begin moving through the tags tree untill you arrive at a figure tag.
  5. Right click on the figure tag and select "Properties"
  6. Fill in the Alternate text and click OK when complete
  7. Continue through all of the figure tags in your document, adding alternate text to each
