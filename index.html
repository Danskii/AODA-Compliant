Making PDFs AODA Compliant with Adobe Acrobat

Hello and welcome. This page was built to to guide you through the process of making PDFs AODA compliant with Adobe Acrobat.

Each page on this site will walk you through the various technical processes for making your PDF AODA compliant.

What is AODA?

The Accessibility for Ontarians with Disabilities Act (AODA) is a law that sets out a process for developing and enforcing accessibility standards. Persons with disabilities and industry representatives work together with the government to develop the standards. Implementing and enforcing these standards will help us reach our goal of an accessible Ontario by 2025.

If you'd like more information on AODA, accessibility, remediation and more, please visit our frequently asked questions page.

This process only requires you to have the most recent version of Adobe Acrobat installed and a PDF to work with.

The end goal of this guide is to ensure your PDF passes the technical requirements for Adobe's Accessibility Check.

After you complete the section simply scroll down to the bottom of the page and click proceed to move to the next step.
